About Amazon

Amazon is a global technology and e-commerce giant headquartered in Seattle, Washington. Founded in 1994, it offers services ranging from online retail and cloud computing (AWS) to AI, streaming, and logistics. Known for its customer obsession and innovation, Amazon is one of the Big Five tech companies, alongside Google, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft.

Amazon Recruitment Process

1. Application and Screening

  • Apply through the Amazon Jobs portal, campus recruitment, or job boards.
  • Optimize your resume with technical projects, leadership experiences, and relevant skills.
  • Referrals from Amazon employees can significantly enhance your application.

2. Interview Rounds

Amazon’s hiring process is rigorous, focusing on technical skills, problem-solving, and alignment with its 16 Leadership Principles.

Online Assessment (OA)

  • Conducted on platforms like HackerRank or Amazon’s internal tools.
  • Includes 2-4 coding problems (algorithms, data structures) and sometimes work simulation or debugging tasks.
  • Focus on code efficiency and edge cases.

Technical Interviews (4-6 rounds)

  • Each round lasts 45-60 minutes, conducted virtually or onsite.
  • Topics: Data Structures (Arrays, Trees, Graphs), Algorithms, System Design, OOP, AWS Concepts.
  • Expect whiteboard coding, system design (for senior roles), and behavioral questions tied to Leadership Principles.

Bar Raiser Interview

  • A unique Amazon round conducted by a trained Bar Raiser to ensure high hiring standards.
  • Assesses cultural fit, leadership potential, and technical depth.

Final Review

  • Interview feedback is evaluated by a hiring committee for a fair decision.
  • Final discussions cover role, team fit, and compensation.

Amazon Interview Preparation

Preparation Tips

  • Practice LeetCode Medium and Hard problems, focusing on arrays, graphs, and dynamic programming.
  • Study system design (e.g., designing scalable APIs, databases) and AWS basics (e.g., EC2, S3).
  • Prepare behavioral responses using the STAR method, aligning with Amazon’s Leadership Principles (e.g., Ownership, Dive Deep).
  • Participate in mock interviews on platforms like Interviewing.io or LeetCode Premium.
  • Review time-space complexity and optimize solutions for performance.

Common Interview Questions

Technical Questions:

  • Implement a LRU Cache with O(1) time complexity.
  • Design a scalable notification system for millions of users.
  • Find the kth largest element in an unsorted array.
  • Detect and remove a cycle in a linked list.
  • Explain load balancing in system design with an example.

Behavioral Questions:

  • Tell me about a time you took ownership of a challenging project.
  • Describe a situation where you had to dive deep to solve a problem.
  • How did you handle a disagreement with a teammate? (Bias for Action)
  • Why do you want to work at Amazon?
